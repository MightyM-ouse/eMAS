## 1. Purpose and content status

This document defines the logical content maintained in the eMAS mapping/configuration workbook and the normalized relationships exported to runtime JSON.

It is the canonical logical data catalogue for the workbook. The workbook may group related Excel Tables on a practical number of worksheets, but the entities, stable identifiers, controlled values and relationships defined here shall remain explicit.

The logical model is Effective. Detailed regulatory rules, authority relationships, format-specific folder/file content, effort weights, confidence weights and exception-role values remain subject to the approved SME review workflow before they become Effective configuration content.

## 2. Global design rules

1. Fixed technical headers and stable Excel table names are protected.
2. Finite value sets use `Value_Lists`.
3. Multi-value relationships use link tables, not comma-separated cells.
4. Rule identity, phases, conditions and outputs remain separate.
5. Findings and recommendations remain separate.
6. Evaluation status, RAG and provenance remain separate.
7. Editable `IsActive` is not used for lifecycle control.
8. Runtime eligibility is calculated.
9. `Rule_Index` is generated by VBA.
10. Controlled runtime export includes Effective content only.
11. PowerShell does not read the workbook.
12. Illustrative examples do not become approved regulatory content without required review evidence.

## 48. Export inclusion rules

Controlled export includes:

- Effective master data and relationships valid on the effective date;
- Effective runtime-eligible rules;
- required controlled values and catalogues;
- required findings, recommendations and policies referenced by included rules;
- approved report terminology;
- configuration and compatibility metadata.

Controlled export excludes:

- Draft, InReview, Superseded and Retired runtime entities;
- workbook formulas, comments and UI-only text;
- project-specific accepted exceptions;
- credentials and secrets;
- validation rows not required by the runtime contract;
- internal VBA source.

## 49. Referential-integrity requirements

Validation shall cover at minimum:

- rule to phase;
- rule to condition group and condition;
- rule to output and finding;
- finding to recommendation link;
- condition to field and operator;
- master-data relationship endpoints;
- superseded to superseding rule;
- exception policy to eligible finding;
- alias to canonical field or value;
- report terminology to approved codes.

## 50. Content approval and SME boundary

The following require documented Regulatory SME review before Effective status:

- new authority or jurisdiction values;
- new technical standards or regional implementations;
- authority/region/standard relationships;
- regulatory folder, file or XML expectations;
- regulatory classification indicators and evidence strength;
- procedure-context interpretation.

The following require Migration/Product Owner approval before Effective status:

- effort weights and thresholds;
- confidence weights;
- migration-scenario treatment;
- decision thresholds;
- exception approver roles and evidence requirements.

An LLM may draft content for review but shall not mark it Effective or fabricate the required evidence.

## 51. Acceptance criteria

The logical catalogue is satisfied when:

1. all entities have stable identifiers and defined fields;
2. relationships are explicit and validated;
3. classification dimensions are normalized;
4. regional implementation and technical standard are separate;
5. ASMF is ProcedureContext;
6. evaluation status, RAG and provenance are separate;
7. findings and recommendations are separate;
8. lifecycle and supersession are explicit;
9. thresholds and effort bands are structurally testable;
10. project exceptions remain outside the master workbook;
11. JSON mapping matches the approved Runtime JSON Contract;
12. content requiring SME approval cannot become Effective without evidence.
